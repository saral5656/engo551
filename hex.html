Colours in Hexadecimal

In the modern era monitors use red, green and blue light that are perceived by the cones within our human eyes. Thus the additive RGB colour system is used where 0 means that there is no light of a certain colour range, while 255 means that there is 100% light of a certain range. This describes the RGB codes.

Hexadecimal is used to describe the colour where six digits represent the amount of red, green and blue. Some common examples are as shown below along with their CSS names.
